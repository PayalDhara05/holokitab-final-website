But before you make any decision, please focus on the word “Too much”. The pandemic has changed the world and hit the traditional education system at its core. Schools have been temporarily closed,and we all, in some way, have moved to eLearning. eLearning was there before, but the pace has grown. It's here to stay.

Healthy Screen Time: Time restrictions for children

When we talk about healthy screen time, it's mostly referred to as timing, for how long is it ideal for a child towatch the screen (mobiles/TV/computer/tab..etc.). While limiting time is a crucial factor, making the screen time educational can actually make it healthy. According to experts:

1) Children aged below 18 months should avoid any screen time except video calls with family.
2) Children aged 18-24 months can have a minimumeducational screen time, provided there's someone to look after them when they are using mobile/watching TV.
3) For children aged 2-6 ,1-2hour on a weekday and 3 hours ontheweekend is good.

Educational Screen Time -Doing it the healthy way

With proper supervision,i.e.,focusing on academics and limiting screen time, you can make your child's screen time healthy. Engaging children in educational screen time can help them in many ways:

1) Improve motor skills and coordination
2) Gamified learning has a powerful impact on the brain: Faster and better understandability
3) Help in improving communication.
